AK-15 MOD1 NOT RECOMMENDED
The stat and Skill 1 upgrades are obviously nice, but you would be missing out on so much nuclear potential by stopping here.		 Just when you thought AK-15 could not be any more angry, here she is with her upgrade, to achieve MAXIMUM ANGERYNESS!!! AK-15 MOD comes with a few interesting gimmicks, but all you really need to know is she can pump out absolutely ludicrous amounts of burst damage.

Her new Skill 2, Blood-Curdling Howl, is a passive skill that coincides with her first skill. Whenever she monitors more than 5 targets at once or loses a dummy link, she will unleash a retaliation strike against the highest Damage enemy. This retaliation deals a staggering 25x Damage that scales with enemy links and ignores HP Shields and Armour. It’s even doubled while she has Rage active, though since this is still on a 15% activation rate, it’s not something you can rely on always being in effect. There are more benefits to this Skill but they’re all defensive in nature and mostly gimmicky and irrelevant.

You may think this kind of makes her akin to an AR version of Carcano M91/38, and well it kind of does. Yeah it’s more complex and relies on there being enough enemies in the enemy group to activate the retaliation, but her ability to just delete the highest Damage target immediately is extremely useful. On top of that, she also remains a strong AR in general and becomes even better thanks to stat upgrades, improved Skill 1, and her Special Equipment. While MOD2 is functional, she really benefits from MOD3 because of her extremely strong Special Equipment.

EM-2 MOD1 NOT RECOMMENDED
Some simple generic buffs to her overall performance won’t change things for EM-2.		 Ah, the EM-2. The best British rifle that ultimately never came to be. At last she gets her vindication, as she gets her Neural Upgrade before M16 does! EM-2 MOD actually flips her niche entirely around, changing from a general mobbing AR that can quickly dispatch a couple of foes to a respectable Single Target damage dealer.

Her new Skill 2 is Resentful Pursuit. When she fires her shots from Skill 1, EM-2 will mark the targets she hits as a “Smash target”. Her normal attacks will always focus on a Smash target and deal 40% more damage to them. Additionally, if a Smash target is killed, she receives a S1 cooldown reduction. Note that her Skill 1 will continue to be random targeting even while a Smash target is still present.

A continuous 40% Damage bonus to her normal attacks is definitely nothing to scoff at. With this active on the right target, EM-2 will be dishing out a lot of damage. Ideally you want to use her in scenarios where there is a big clear threat but not much else. This way, the odds of her hitting the intended target with her Skill 1 is much better. Even better if said target is the only target, ensuring that all her Skill 1 shots always go to them.

All in all, a pretty huge improvement for this plucky rifle ahead of her time. EM-2 now has herself a solid niche and does it very well. Nothing about her is a must have though, but if you choose to upgrade, use her well and EM-2 won’t let you down. MOD3 is recommended here, since her Special Equipment is an improved Focus Chip with no Rate of Fire penalty and an extra 10 Accuracy and 5 Damage.

G11 MOD1 NOT RECOMMENDED
Leaving G11 here would mean missing out on what makes her special.		 G11 is one of the laziest members of Griffin, but there is sure as hell nothing lazy about this upgrade. G11 MOD boasts extremely high burst damage capabilities letting her tear through some enemies far more quickly than most other ARs.

Her Skill 2, Stance of the Assaulter, causes her to deal an extra hit of damage whenever she hits the same enemy 3 times. This extra hit deals 2% of the target’s max HP, capped at 300% of G11’s Damage stat. It is affected by armour, but does ignore HP shields. We know G11’s Rate of Fire is monstrous, so she is going to be putting out a lot of these extra hits effortlessly, not to mention that her Skill 1 makes her shoot 3 times per volley. In practice, her DPS increase is phenomenal.

Statwise, G11 primarily improves her Damage stat, which was the stat most in need of boosting. Her new tiles can boost 2 SMGs at once now and her improved Skill 1 lasts for half a second longer. Much like her Skill 2, these buffs are appreciated.

If you have G11 in your arsenal, this is definitely an upgrade to pick up. While ARs are falling out of favour in the meta as time goes on, they are still used from time to time, and G11 is a strong option for this but comes at a much higher cost than ST AR-15, who has similar long-term performance with her SPEQ-Set and does not cost FCCs. If you do mod her, she is one of the only 5 star MODs which I can recommend taking to MOD3. Her Special Equipment is exceptional, it is a chip replacement that is far better suited for her and definitely worth the cost.

AN-94 MOD1 SUPER BUDGET
A level 110 AN-94 MOD1 gets a substantial upgrade to her performance for a good price.		 AN-94 is a bit of an unfortunate case sadly. She’s not a bad AR by any means, but her upgrade isn’t all that exciting. Unfortunately, her Skill 2 is mostly occupied by AK-12 synergy which can be ignored, while otherwise you find a costly but still good AR.

MOD1 is overall a fairly nice upgrade. She gets more stats and her Skill 1 lasts for an additional second. Not much else to say, straightforward and to the point.

MOD2 gives her Trailing Wolf Shadows, which carries a lot of synergy with DEFY that, with the release of AK-12 MOD, can finally be used to some effect! It grants all DEFY members a mark every time she switches target or activates Skill 1. The marks stack up to 3 times, lasting for 5 seconds. AK-12’s damage output will benefit from the Crit Rate conversion into Crit Damage, but the conversion of RoF into Damage is not worth it. Overall though, the generic DEFY bonus benefits her much more.

If you’re going to upgrade AN-94, you may as well go the full way to MOD3. Thankfully, AN-94 does have a nice Special Equipment to go along with the upgrade. That being said, each level of the upgrade does potentially offer something: MOD1 is relatively cheap and offers some nice boosts, while MOD2 does offer a good DPS boost and the synergy with AK-12 MOD.

AK-12 MOD1 NOT RECOMMENDED
AK-12 gets some nice buffs to her base form, Including an increase to her base Crit Rate, but it is advised to go further. Stopping here is not worth it.		 After waiting seemingly forever, AK-12 finally has her neural upgrade. Now that it’s here, I can confidently say… It’s pretty good. Nothing meta defining, but a good AR in the right situation, who can put out some good damage when paired with AN-94 MOD especially.
With her new Skill 2, Alpha’s Instinct, every time one of her attacks crits, she applies a ‘Marked’ stack to the target. This stack reduces the enemies armor and evasion by 1 and also causes them to take 1 more point of damage per attack. Doesn’t sound like a lot, but there is no limit to how many stacks can be applied to any target (outside of stacks simply starting to naturally expire). In addition to this, DEFY members benefit twice from the flat damage taken debuff.

As you can likely surmise, this skill very much points AK-12 towards a single target oriented role. In something like a boss fight, she can solely focus herself on one target and really begin to crank up the marked stacks on the foe. In more general battles against multiple targets, AK-12 won’t get the opportunity to make her stacks actually matter all that much.
This gets even better when you throw AN-94 into the mix. The extra hits that AN-94 will give AK-12 can crit, and when they do, they will also apply a Marked stack. As such, AK-12 applies stacks so much faster with AN-94, that using her without AN-94 is a DPS loss. In addition to this, AK-12 will overcap her Crit Rate during her Skill 1, meaning AN-94 will convert 30% of her Crit Rate into 50% Crit Damage for a substantial personal damage boost.
Her tiles now cover her column as well, which goes along with her buffed Skill 1 which now provides a number of small buffs to allies on her tiles. Unfortunately the tilebuff still doesn’t buff ARs, which is a shame considering it would’ve been a strong Damage and RoF buff.

All in all, AK-12 sets out to do single target well and she does fulfill that role. In a team with AN-94 and Ribeyrolles for example, their output will be similar to that of the SCAR sisters in optimal situations. There are some big caveats: She is extremely expensive, requiring two 6 ☆ upgrades to work well. She is not very good against groups of enemies, due to the constant need to stack debuffs on each new enemy. And she's a front-targeting AR, unable to hit backline.
First, make sure to upgrade AN-94 as well, and second: MOD3 is the overall recommendation. Her Special Equipment is pretty neat, being a stronger Focus Chip and allowing her to basically become near invincible during her Skill 1. Do note though that she cannot get directly buffed while she’s in the invincible period, but buffs that check the tiles, such as Ribeyrolles, will still work. Otherwise, you may need to use AK-12 in manual skill mode if you use this SPEQ in order to ensure she still can gain full benefits from buff.

RO635 MOD1 BUDGET
The stats and skill improvements are a good upgrade to RO635. While you should go further, leaving her here for a short while is fine if you’re new or low on FCCs.		 RO635 MOD is something quite interesting. Have you ever wanted to see a doll that can theoretically stare an enemy right in the face and give it the middle finger as its attacks bounce off her with not a scratch dealt? That… typically won’t happen, but RO635 could do it if RNG goes her way.

Her Skill 2, Righteous Judgement, allows her to potentially dodge a lot of incoming attacks. When she is hit by an attack that deals less than one link’s worth of HP (before linksplash), she has a base 20% to roll a second evasion check that also works on surehit attacks. The chance to roll the second evasion check increases with the amount of (certain types of) debuffs on an enemy, with each layer granting RO an additional 10% chance. When Skill 1 is active, the base chance to roll the second check goes up to 40%.

The main downside to this skill is how it can be potentially ignored. However, there are very few enemies which would actually deal enough damage in a single instance to ignore it, and typically you wouldn’t be having RO fight these enemies in the first place anyway. Besides that, it is still pure RNG how much this skill will protect her. Sometimes it will work in her favour, sometimes it will not. When we’ve now got other upgrades that offer consistent protection for the tanks, RO has fallen behind.

Besides this, RO gets your typical suite of upgrades. Statwise, she focuses primarily on HP and Evasion upgrades to bring her default tankiness up even further. Her Skill 1 gets a second debuff and her tiles also get slightly stronger.

With 2 layers of evasion checks and really good stats, with proper setup, RO635 MOD does serve as an effective tank. However, there are other SMGs that tank better with less effort, most notably Suomi MOD. MOD2 is her best stopping point, you’re here for the skill and everything else is bonus. MOD3 is a good upgrade, but for 5 FCCs you really want more than just 1 point of HP and an improved T4 Exo.

…Actually, MOD3 does have her incredible art. She looks so cool and mature and comforting… Is this definitely RO MOD? Seems more like RO MOM to me.

Type 79 MOD1 NOT RECOMMENDED
The simple upgrades to her stats, Skill 1, and tiles are not enough to carry Type 79.		 You know how 100 Shiki and RO635 suffer in priority due to the existence of Suomi MOD? Well, here’s another SMG that can join the “Good upgrade but Suomi literally exists” club! Type 79 is the next 5 star SMG to join that prestigious club, coming with a good upgrade that boosts her survivability but with some caveats that hinder her compared to the tanking queen.

Her new Skill 2, Fireproof Fortress, grants her two separate bonuses. The more notable of the two: She grants herself a 15% HP shield the moment she takes any amount of damage to her HP. This is on a 3 second cooldown, so if her shields last long enough, she can theoretically take only a single instance of damage every 3 seconds, maybe even longer if the enemies are not hitting hard enough to break it before the cooldown is up. The other bonus is a stacking Damage and Crit Rate boost that occurs every 1.5 seconds she doesn’t take any HP damage.

This is definitely a good survivability tool, but the problem is that it’s her only survival tool. She does have a Flashbang Grenade with a 4.5 second stun, but many modern enemies flat out cannot be stunned. This Flashbang does get a buff in her upgrade, but it’s purely offensive leaning, inflicting Vulnerability if it only hits a single foe and also granting 90% Night Vision during night battles. For the most part, this skill can be considered a Night Battle utility.

Overall, Type 79 MOD has the components of making a good tank SMG and she does perform her duties well enough. However, what she offers simply isn’t enough for her to keep up with Suomi MOD. If you do upgrade her, MOD2 is the primarily recommended level. That being said, her Special Equipment does reduce her Flashbang cooldown by 25%, which effectively makes her Night Vision buff have permanent uptime.

100 Shiki MOD1 NOT RECOMMENDED
This upgrade is decent and enhances her base form to more usable levels, but lacks a lot of the potential power of her upgrade.		 100 Shiki, after so long, can finally make a true name for herself outside of the JP server with her new fancy Neural Upgrade. The core ideas behind 100 Shiki remain the same: If her HP Shield breaks, she will receive defensive buffs, if her HP Shield expires, she will receive offensive buffs. An interesting idea for a hybrid SMG but one that never really paid off.

Now, she has much more substantial defensive benefits when her shield breaks thanks to her Skill 2, Sakura Blizzard, which prevents the first instance of damage that would kill one of her links and grants an HP shield which gets stronger as her HP drops. Combined with a much reduced ICD and Cooldown on Skill 2, this greatly enhances her defensive capabilities to become slightly stronger than that of RO635 MOD (without setup).

100 Shiki’s main downside is not even one really with herself, but more with her competition. Suomi MOD boasts incredible survivability that far exceeds 100 Shiki at the exact same cost in terms of resources. Nevertheless, 100 Shiki MOD is strong on her own merits and worthy of consideration. MOD2 is where she gets the bulk of her new power from, with MOD3 being an optional upgrade thanks to her nice but ultimately unneeded Special Equipment.
